overview
the idea.

electronics students spend a startling amount of time looking up resistor color codes. there's a chart on every wall — but charts don't help when the room is dim, the bands are worn, or the colors look ambiguous through human eyes.

resistor lab turns the phone camera into a real-time decoder. the model detects the resistor body, segments the bands, classifies each color, and computes the resistance value — all on-device with tensorflow lite.

built as the bangkit academy 2024 machine learning capstone. i led a team of five through dataset collection, model training, and android integration.

how it works

camera → bands → ohms.

01

dataset & augmentation

self-collected dataset of 2,400 images across resistor types and lighting conditions. heavy augmentation — rotation, blur, color jitter, white-balance shifts — to handle the messy classroom environments the app would actually live in.

labelimg albumentations
02

two-stage model

first stage: localize the resistor body with a lightweight detector. second stage: a color-band classifier that runs only inside the cropped region. splitting the problem made the small classifier dramatically more reliable.

tensorflow cnn
03

on-device, offline

quantized to int8 and converted to tflite so the whole pipeline runs locally on a mid-range android. no network calls, no privacy concerns, works in a basement lab.

tflite int8
04

android ui

camera viewfinder with live overlay rectangles, a stable readout once the prediction settles for ~5 frames, and a small history list so you can re-check the last reading without re-aiming.
